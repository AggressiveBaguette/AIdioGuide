<speak version="1.0" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2001/10/synthesis" xmlns:mstts="http://www.w3.org/2001/mstts" xml:lang="en-US"><voice name="en-US-JennyMultilingualNeural">The piazza in front of you is unusually large for this neighborhood. In a medieval city built on the logic of dense, narrow streets, open spaces this size do not appear by accident. They are made, either by demolition or by design, for a specific purpose.  This one was the arena for the <lang xml:lang='it-IT'>calcio storico</lang>, the historic Florentine football, a game played between the four city quarters with teams of twenty-seven men, in full period dress, combining elements of football, rugby, and organized fighting. The rules, such as they were, permitted punching, wrestling, and most forms of physical contact short of outright killing. It was not a metaphor for violence. It was violence, with a ball and a referee.  The game has a precise political moment attached to it. On the seventeenth of February 1530, while Charles V's imperial troops had Florence under siege and artillery was firing on the city, the Florentine patriciate organized a match here, in full view of the bombardment. The message was deliberate: we play while you shoot. It was one of the more extraordinary acts of collective defiance in the city's history.  After the siege ended and the Medici returned to power, the <lang xml:lang='it-IT'>calcio storico</lang> was absorbed into the court spectacle apparatus. <lang xml:lang='it-IT'>Alessandro de' Medici</lang>, then <lang xml:lang='it-IT'>Cosimo I</lang>, participated in or presided over the matches. A game that had expressed republican contempt for an occupying army became a ducal entertainment. In 1580, Giovanni de' <lang xml:lang='it-IT'>Bardi</lang>, the same man who founded the <lang xml:lang='it-IT'>Camerata fiorentina</lang> and helped invent opera, wrote the first formal treatise codifying the rules, explicitly reserving the game for gentlemen.  The popular pressure that had once filled this square with genuine civic fury was repackaged as aristocratic leisure. <break time="2s"/> Florence has always known how to keep the form while emptying the content.
