The lower register is covered in hexagonal panels, each one depicting a human activity: agriculture, weaving, navigation, medicine, and there, among them, a figure called <phoneme alphabet='ipa' ph='dʒiˈonitus'>Gionitus</phoneme>, a man studying the stars. <break time="1s"/> Astronomy. Carved into the base of a bell tower attached to a cathedral, commissioned not by the Church, but by the Commune of Florence.  That distinction matters more than it might seem. When the city appointed <lang xml:lang='it-IT'>Giotto</lang> as master builder on the twelfth of April 1334, the appointment was explicitly political. The Commune wanted the most famous living artist in Europe attached to its most visible civic project. Not the bishop's project. The city's. And when it came time to decide what to carve into the stone at eye level, where every citizen and every visitor would pass and look, the Commune chose to celebrate human knowledge: the crafts, the sciences, the practical arts by which civilization sustains itself.  This is a manifesto in marble, displayed in the most prominent religious space in the city, funded by the same institution that ran the courts and collected the taxes. The medieval separation between sacred and civic authority, which was supposed to be absolute, is simply not visible here. The tower announces that Florence's government considers human reason to be a subject worthy of monumental stone.  Worth noting: what you are looking at are copies. The original bas-reliefs were removed and transferred to the <lang xml:lang='it-IT'>Museo dell'Opera del Duomo</lang>, where the actual fourteenth-century stone now sits in a controlled environment. The panels on the tower today are modern reproductions, which means this manifesto, like the baptistery doors, is now being broadcast by a facsimile. <break time="2s"/>  Florence has spent five centuries building monuments to itself, and the last few decades quietly moving the originals indoors. There is probably a lesson in that, though the city has not yet carved it anywhere.</voice></speak>
